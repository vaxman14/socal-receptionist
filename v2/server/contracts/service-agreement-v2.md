This Service Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into between **SoCal
Receptionist, LLC**, a California limited liability company located in Murrieta,
California ("Provider," "we," "us"), and the business identified in the
onboarding account and signature block below ("Client," "you").

By electronically signing this Agreement, you agree to the following terms.

## 1. Services

Provider operates an AI-powered virtual receptionist that answers inbound text
messages, and where enabled inbound calls, on behalf of the Client. The service
responds to general inquiries, shares business information the Client supplies,
captures leads, and assists with appointment scheduling. The specific features
activated for the Client are those reflected in the Client's account at the time
service begins.

## 2. Term and Commencement

This Agreement begins on the date the Client electronically signs it and
continues on a month-to-month basis until terminated under Section 10. Service
provisioning begins only after this Agreement is signed and the Client's first
payment or trial enrollment is completed.

## 3. Fees and Payment

The Client agrees to pay the setup fee and recurring monthly subscription fee
presented in the Client's account at the time of enrollment. Recurring fees are
billed in advance through the Client's chosen payment method. A trial period, if
offered, requires a valid payment method and converts to paid service at the end
of the trial unless cancelled. Fees are non-refundable except where required by
law. Provider may change fees with at least thirty (30) days' notice; continued
use after a fee change constitutes acceptance.

## 4. Client Responsibilities

The Client agrees to:

- Provide accurate, current business information (hours, services, pricing,
  policies) and keep it updated;
- Use the service only for lawful business communications;
- Comply with all applicable laws governing communication with the Client's own
  customers, including the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), the CAN-SPAM
  Act, and carrier messaging rules — the Client is responsible for having a
  lawful basis to contact its customers;
- Not use the service to send spam, unsolicited marketing, or prohibited content
  (including content related to illegal goods, hate, or fraud);
- Cooperate with carrier-required registration (A2P 10DLC) by supplying truthful
  business-identity information.

## 5. Messaging Compliance

US application-to-person SMS requires carrier registration (A2P 10DLC) that is
outside Provider's control and typically takes several business days to clear.
Provider will submit registration on the Client's behalf using information the
Client supplies, but does not guarantee a specific approval timeline or outcome.
The service enforces opt-in consent and STOP/HELP keyword handling for the
Client's customers.

## 6. AI-Generated Responses

Responses are generated by an automated AI system and may be inaccurate,
incomplete, or outdated. The AI is not a licensed professional and does not
provide legal, medical, financial, or other professional advice. The Client is
responsible for reviewing how the service represents the Client's business and
for the accuracy of the underlying information the Client provides.

## 7. Usage Limits and Fair Use

The service is intended for genuine communication between the Client's
customers and the Client's business. To protect service quality and control
operating cost, Provider enforces automated usage limits, currently:

- A maximum of ten (10) calls per day to a Client line from any single phone
  number;
- A maximum call duration of ten (10) minutes, after which the call may be
  ended automatically with notice to the caller;
- A maximum of thirty (30) inbound text messages per day from any single
  sender.

Callers or senders who exceed a limit receive a courteous notice and are
directed to contact the business directly or try again later. The AI assistant
is restricted to the Client's business purposes. It will refuse attempts to
misuse it, including attempts to extract its underlying instructions or to use
it for unrelated tasks such as generating content or code, and these
protections apply to all configurations, including custom prompts supplied by
the Client. Provider monitors usage for abuse and may adjust limits or block
abusive numbers to protect the service. Provider may update these limits from
time to time; limits will not be reduced in a way that materially degrades the
service without notice to the Client.

## 8. Data and Privacy

Each party will handle personal information in accordance with applicable law.
Provider processes customer phone numbers and message content to deliver the
service, as described in the SoCal Receptionist Privacy Policy. The Client
authorizes Provider to use third-party processors, including Twilio (messaging)
and OpenAI (AI responses), to operate the service.

## 9. Intellectual Property

Provider retains all rights in the service, its software, and its AI
configuration. The Client retains all rights in its own business information and
customer data. The Client grants Provider a limited license to use that
information solely to operate the service for the Client.

## 10. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, effective at the end of
the current billing period, by giving notice through the Client's account or in
writing. Provider may suspend or terminate service immediately for non-payment,
unlawful use, abuse, or a carrier or regulatory directive. Upon termination,
the Client's phone number handling will be released and access will end.

## 11. Disclaimers and Limitation of Liability

The service is provided "as is" without warranties of any kind. Provider does
not warrant uninterrupted or error-free service. To the fullest extent permitted
by law, Provider's total liability arising out of or relating to this Agreement
will not exceed the fees paid by the Client in the three (3) months preceding the
claim, and Provider will not be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential,
or punitive damages.

## 12. Indemnification

The Client will indemnify and hold Provider harmless from claims arising out of
the Client's business information, the Client's communications with its own
customers, or the Client's violation of law or this Agreement.

## 13. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of California. Any dispute
will be resolved in the state or federal courts located in Riverside County,
California, and the parties consent to that jurisdiction.

## 14. Electronic Signature

The parties agree this Agreement may be signed electronically and that an
electronic signature has the same legal effect as a handwritten signature under
the federal ESIGN Act and the California Uniform Electronic Transactions Act.
The Client consents to transact electronically and to receive this Agreement and
related records in electronic form.

## 15. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with the Privacy Policy and Terms of Use referenced in
the Client's account, is the entire agreement between the parties regarding the
service and supersedes prior discussions. If any provision is held
unenforceable, the remainder stays in effect.
